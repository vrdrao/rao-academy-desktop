## Background — what FR-1 broke and what Venkat ruled

FR-1 was asked to make Try Again restore first-attempt state. It over-reached
and retired the wrong-answer mark (✕) entirely: a child answering wrong now
sees no indication of what they picked, and the whyWrong bubble has nothing to
attach to.

**The seven rulings this brief implements (from HANDOFF-24, binding):**

1. **✕ appears on a wrong answer; ✕ clears on Try Again.** That was always
   the intent.
2. **✕ on wrong selections only** — never on a correct selection, never on an
   unselected option.
3. **Multi-select: EVERY wrong selection gets a ✕.** This deliberately
   REVERSES the signed-off Brief 7.6 call recorded in HANDOFF-7 ("✕ on every
   selected option"). Overruled chat-side: marking only the wrong selections
   does not leak sufficiency — a child seeing two ✕ among four selections
   still does not know the unmarked two are enough.
4. **Fill-blanks get NO ✕ glyph.** A wrong entry stays visible in the child's
   own text with a soft red tint on border and text. Only wrong blanks tint;
   correct blanks are untouched. **The typed value is NEVER cleared** —
   erasing it reads as punishment. A ✕ is a badge stuck on a thing; a typed
   answer is the child's handwriting.
5. **TWO ATTEMPTS IS THE CAP.** Second wrong attempt → the question locks, no
   Try Again is offered, and the walkthrough OPENS AUTOMATICALLY. No child
   grinds on one question, and no decision is demanded of a child who has
   just failed twice.
6. **No green at walkthrough open.** Green appears exactly twice ever: at the
   correct moment, and at the walkthrough's FINAL reveal. Greening the answer
   when the walkthrough opens kills the explanation.
7. **Law 6 is amended, not violated.** "Never auto-opened" (in `BRIEFS.md`,
   the `rao-card.js` header, and the LOCK-ON-OPEN guard) is re-pointed to
   assert what now matters: opening by EITHER path — child taps "Walk me
   through it" or the second-wrong auto-open — locks the question immediately
   and records `solved-with-help`.

## Design laws that bind every commit

- Green exactly twice (ruling 6 above).
- Help accumulates: nothing the card has told the child is ever removed.
  Try Again clears ✕ marks/tints ONLY — hints, bubbles, and action history
  stay. Attempt counters are never reset by Try Again.
- No-repaint: prior rendered elements are never touched after render; new
  state arrives in new nodes. The ✕ marks and tints are the sanctioned
  exception ONLY in the clearing direction on Try Again — they are marks, not
  content.
